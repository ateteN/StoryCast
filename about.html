About StoryCast

StoryCast is an independent, digital storytelling platform dedicated to surfacing the stories that mainstream media overlooks, suppresses, or simply never finds. We produce long-form audio documentaries, written investigations, and multimedia features that give voice to researchers, whistleblowers, and ordinary people who have something extraordinary to say.

Founded in 2024, StoryCast has published more than 120 stories across topics ranging from space exploration and fringe science to geopolitical anomalies and declassified government programmes. We do not take advertising from government bodies or political parties. Our independence is non-negotiable.

Our Mission

We believe that every person, regardless of background, geography, or ability, deserves access to compelling, well-researched stories. StoryCast is built on three core values:

  • Inclusivity: We strive to make every piece of content accessible to the widest possible audience, including people with visual, auditory, cognitive, and motor disabilities.
  • Transparency: All sources are cited. All corrections are published openly. We stand behind our journalism.
  • Diversity of voice: We actively seek contributors from under-represented communities and actively avoid the homogenised perspective that dominates corporate media.

Our long-term goal is to become the most trusted home for alternative and investigative storytelling on the web—a place where curiosity is celebrated and critical thinking is encouraged.

Accessibility at StoryCast

StoryCast is committed to meeting WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards across all pages and content formats. Below is a plain-language summary of the accessibility features we have implemented.

  • Captions for Audio & Video

    Every audio documentary and video feature published on StoryCast includes a synchronised caption track in WebVTT format. Captions are enabled by default so that deaf or hard-of-hearing users receive the same experience without additional steps.

  • Full Text Transcripts

    In addition to captions, each audio story page contains a complete, timestamped text transcript. This benefits users who prefer to read, users with slow connections who cannot stream audio, and search engines that help new readers discover our content.

  • Keyboard Navigation

    All interactive elements—navigation links, audio controls, form fields, and buttons—are fully operable using a keyboard alone (Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Space). A visible skip-navigation link at the top of every page allows keyboard users to jump directly to the main content, bypassing repeated navigation.

  • High-Contrast Design

    StoryCast uses a dark-background colour scheme with high-contrast text that meets or exceeds the WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratio of 4.5 : 1 for normal text and 3 : 1 for large text. All interactive states (focus, hover, visited) provide visible, distinct indicators.

  • Semantic HTML for Screen Readers

    Every page is built with semantic HTML5 elements— <header>, <nav>, <main>, <article>, <section>, <figure>, and <footer>—so that screen readers such as NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver can accurately describe the structure and hierarchy of each page to their users.

  • Descriptive Alt Text

    Every image on StoryCast includes a meaningful alt attribute that conveys the information or context a sighted user would gain from viewing the image. Decorative images are given an empty alt="" so screen readers skip them.

  • ARIA Landmarks & Labels

    Where native HTML semantics are insufficient, we use ARIA attributes such as aria-label, aria-labelledby, aria-describedby, and aria-current to provide additional context to assistive technologies.

If you encounter any accessibility barrier on StoryCast, please contact us and we will resolve the issue as quickly as possible.

Get Involved

StoryCast is more than a publishing outlet—it is a community. Here are the ways you can participate, contribute, and shape the stories we tell:

  • Submit a tip: If you have information about a story you believe the public needs to know, use our secure contact form below. All submissions are treated as confidential unless you tell us otherwise.
  • Pitch a story: Are you a freelance journalist, researcher, or subject-matter expert? We welcome pitches for audio documentaries and written investigations. Include a brief synopsis, key sources, and your credentials.
  • Share our content: Every StoryCast story is free to share under a Creative Commons Attribution licence. Spread the word, link back to us, and credit the original author.
  • Support us: StoryCast is reader-funded. If you value independent journalism, consider making a one-time or recurring contribution through our support page.
