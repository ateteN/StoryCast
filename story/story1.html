The Moon Landing Was Faked?

By James Holloway — Published 15 February 2026

Audio Documentary

About This Story

On 20 July 1969, NASA broadcast live footage of astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walking on the surface of the Moon. For most people, it was the greatest achievement in human history. But for a persistent and growing community of researchers, the images raise more questions than they answer.

In this documentary, investigative journalist James Holloway sits down with three independent analysts who have spent decades studying the original 16 mm footage, NASA's own photographic archive, and recently declassified communications between Houston and the Apollo crew. Their findings, they claim, suggest that at least portions of the broadcast were staged—whether to conceal technical failures, Cold War vulnerabilities, or something altogether more extraordinary.

StoryCast presents both sides of the debate. We spoke with space historians, former NASA engineers, and independent physicists to give listeners a complete picture. Decide for yourself.

The following is a full transcript of the audio documentary above. Timestamps refer to the audio playback position.

[00:00] – Introduction

James Holloway: Welcome to StoryCast. I'm James Holloway, and tonight we ask a question that has haunted conspiracy theorists, armchair historians, and even a handful of NASA insiders for more than fifty years: did humanity really land on the Moon in 1969, or was the greatest moment in television history also the greatest deception ever staged?

[02:30] – The Flag Problem

James Holloway: One of the earliest doubts raised by sceptics concerns the American flag planted by Armstrong and Aldrin. In multiple frames of footage, the flag appears to ripple and wave. On the Moon, there is no atmosphere—and therefore no wind. How could a flag wave in a vacuum?

Dr. Carmen Ruiz (Independent Physicist): The flag had a horizontal rod sewn into its top seam, designed precisely so it would hang flat in the absence of air. The movement you see is residual motion from the astronauts handling the pole. It damps out quickly—exactly as physics predicts. There is no paradox here.

[08:15] – The Shadow Anomalies

James Holloway: Analyst Ray Kowalski has spent twelve years examining the shadows in Apollo surface photographs. He joins us now from his studio in Seattle.

Ray Kowalski (Photo Analyst): What you see in these images is shadows pointing in slightly different directions. On Earth, with a single light source—the Sun—all shadows must be parallel. These are not. That strongly implies multiple studio light sources.

Dr. Carmen Ruiz: The lunar surface is not flat. It is uneven, cratered, and slopes at many angles. When terrain is uneven, parallel light rays cast shadows in different apparent directions depending on the local slope. This is basic photogrammetry, not evidence of a studio.

[18:00] – Closing Thoughts

James Holloway: As with so many great mysteries, the evidence cuts both ways. What is undeniable is that Apollo 11 changed the world— whether every frame of footage is genuine or not. I'm James Holloway. Thank you for listening to StoryCast.
